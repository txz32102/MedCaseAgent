---
name: care_case_report
description: Apply compact CARE-style case report structure and content checks.
when_to_use: Use when planning, drafting, or refining a clinical case report.
---
Use a conventional case report structure when the source does not require another order:

1. Title
2. Abstract
3. Introduction
4. Case Presentation
5. Discussion
6. Patient Perspective, Ethics, Consent, Conflicts, Funding, or References only when supported by the source.

Content rules:
- Title should identify the central condition or intervention and include "case report".
- Abstract should be concise and citation-free.
- Case Presentation should contain patient information, clinical findings, timeline, diagnostics, interventions, follow-up, outcomes, and tolerability when supplied.
- Discussion should explain clinical reasoning, differential diagnosis, novelty, limitations, and take-away lessons without overstating certainty.
- Do not fabricate patient consent, ethics approval, conflicts of interest, or patient perspective.

---
name: citation_policy
description: Prevent invented literature and unsupported references.
when_to_use: Use whenever references, novelty, background, or literature comparison may appear.
---
Citation rules:
- Use only references, DOIs, PMIDs, URLs, bibliography entries, or verified citation-bank entries present in the supplied context.
- Do not invent authors, article titles, journal names, years, DOIs, PMIDs, guideline names, or reference strings.
- If a verified citation bank is supplied, use relevant entries from that bank for background, diagnostic criteria, management context, and literature comparison.
- If neither the case record nor the verified citation bank contains references, keep background claims general and uncited.
- If a References section is necessary but no verified references or verified citation-bank entries were supplied, state that no verified references were supplied rather than fabricating entries.
- Literature comparison must be conservative: prefer "rare", "unusual", or "limited reports" only when supported by the source.
